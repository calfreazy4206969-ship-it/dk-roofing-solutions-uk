Roofing in Hall Green

Hall Green's housing is predominantly 1930s semi-detached, with hipped concrete tile roofs that were built to a similar specification across most of B28. These roofs age at similar rates — ridge and hip tile mortar tends to dry and crack after 20–30 years, and the concrete tiles themselves can begin to erode and lose their weatherproofing after 40+ years.

Rear additions on these properties are almost always covered with traditional felt, and by now most are due for replacement. We recommend EPDM as the upgrade — it's a single bonded membrane with no joints to fail and a 25–50 year lifespan.

Common roof issues in B28

  • Cracked ridge and hip tile mortar pointing
  • Eroded or porous concrete roof tiles
  • Failed felt flat roofs on rear extensions
  • Hipped roof junction leaks
  • Blocked or sagging plastic guttering
  • Moss and algae growth on north-facing slopes
