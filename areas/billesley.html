Local Knowledge

Roofing in Billesley

Billesley is a well-established residential area of south Birmingham — predominantly 1930s semi-detached properties on well-laid-out streets. The hipped roof design common on these semis is more complex to maintain than a simple gable-end roof: the hip junctions require periodic mortar re-bedding, and the hip tiles are a common point of failure as the original bedding carbonates over time.

The concrete tiles on many Billesley properties were laid in the 1960s–70s as replacements for the original clay. These are now 50–60 years old and increasingly prone to cracking and delamination. We see a mix of single tile replacements and full re-roof projects in the area — and we always give an honest assessment of which is the better option for the specific roof.

Common roof problems in Billesley B13

  • Failed mortar on hipped roof junctions
  • Cracked concrete tiles (1960s–70s replacements)
  • Flat felt extension roofs past lifespan
  • Cast iron gutter corrosion
  • Ridge tile mortar failure
  • Moss on north-facing slopes
