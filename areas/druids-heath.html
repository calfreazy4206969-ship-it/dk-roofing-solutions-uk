Roofing in Druids Heath

Druids Heath was built primarily in the 1960s and early 1970s as a large residential estate on the south-eastern edge of Kings Heath. The properties are predominantly semi-detached and terraced houses built to estate designs of that era — low roof pitches, concrete interlocking tiles, and a high density of flat-roofed garages.

Those original concrete tiles are now 50–60 years old, and many are starting to crack, delaminate or become porous. The flat-roofed garages — originally built with traditional bitumen felt — have largely exceeded their felt lifespan and are prime candidates for EPDM rubber replacement.
