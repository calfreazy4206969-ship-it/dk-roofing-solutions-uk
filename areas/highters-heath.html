Roofing in Highters Heath

Highters Heath is a quiet residential area of southern Birmingham, centred around the Highters Road corridor and the streets running off it. The housing is predominantly 1930s–50s semis and early post-war houses — well-built properties that are now in need of periodic roof maintenance.

The most common call-outs we get in Highters Heath are flat extension roofs that have passed their lifespan, cracked concrete tiles on 1950s–60s roof replacements, and ridge tile mortar that has carbonated and crumbled. Being based here means we know the properties — and can usually quote and schedule repairs much faster than firms coming from further away.

Common roof issues in Highters Heath B14

  • Flat felt roofs on rear extensions past lifespan
  • Cracked concrete tiles
  • Failed ridge mortar
  • Cast iron guttering corrosion
  • Moss build-up on north-facing slopes
  • Blown felt on flat garage roofs
