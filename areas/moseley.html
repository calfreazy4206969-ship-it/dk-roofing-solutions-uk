Roofing in Moseley

Moseley is one of Birmingham's most architecturally distinctive areas — Victorian and Edwardian properties with high-pitched roofs, decorative ridge tiles, multiple chimney stacks and elaborate bargeboards. These properties need roofers who understand the materials and have the experience to work on older clay tile and natural slate roofs without causing further damage.

Rear additions — common throughout B13 — typically have flat felt roofs that are often past their best. EPDM rubber is our go-to recommendation here: it lasts 25–50 years and doesn't need the constant attention that old felt demands.

Common roof issues in B13

  • Slipped or broken natural slate tiles
  • Cracked decorative clay ridge tiles
  • Chimney stack repointing and flaunching
  • Failed lead flashings at chimney and valleys
  • Old felt flat roofs on Victorian rear additions
  • Cast iron guttering pulling away from fascia boards
