Flat Roofing in B14

The rear extensions in Kings Heath are a perfect illustration of the flat roof problem in Birmingham. Many of the kitchen and bathroom extensions built in the 1970s–90s on the Victorian terraces throughout B14 used traditional felt — a material that degrades in UV and typically needs full replacement after 10–15 years.

By now, most of these felt roofs are long overdue. We regularly replace them with EPDM rubber systems — which are seamless, much more resistant to pooling water, and will last the lifetime of the extension.

Being based on Highters Road, we can usually survey and quote flat roof jobs in Kings Heath within a day or two.

Signs your flat roof needs replacing

  • Bubbling or blistering on the felt surface
  • Ponding water that doesn't drain after 48 hours
  • Visible cracks or splits in the covering
  • Damp patches on the ceiling below
  • Felt shrinking away from the upstands
  • More than 15 years old (lifespan exceeded)
