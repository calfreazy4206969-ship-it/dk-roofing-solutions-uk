Flat Roofing in Billesley B13

The rear extensions added to Billesley's 1930s semis from the 1970s onwards — kitchen extensions, bathroom extensions, utility rooms — are almost all flat-roofed, and most were built with traditional 3-layer felt. That felt has long since passed its lifespan on the older extensions. We replace with EPDM rubber — a seamless, UV-stable membrane that suits the low roof pitches involved and lasts 25–50 years.

EPDM is our standard recommendation for Billesley extensions because of its track record on low-pitched roofs and its resistance to the pooling water that's common where falls are shallow. GRP fibreglass is the alternative for roofs with more foot traffic or where a harder finish is preferred.

Signs your flat roof needs attention

  • Bubbling or blistering felt
  • Ponding water after rain
  • Cracks in the covering
  • Damp ceiling below the flat roof
  • Felt lifting from upstands
  • 15+ years old with no replacement
