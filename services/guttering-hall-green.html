Guttering & Fascia Services in Hall Green B28

The uPVC guttering fitted to Hall Green's 1930s semis in the 1980s and 90s is now reaching the end of its life. Cracking joints, failing brackets and sagging runs are the most common issues we see across B28.

uPVC Gutter Replacement

Full replacement of failed uPVC guttering runs — including joints, brackets and corners. We match profiles to existing where needed, or upgrade to a wider system for improved flow on Hall Green's larger roofs.

Fascia & Soffit Replacement

Rotten timber fascia boards are common on Hall Green's 1930s properties. We replace with low-maintenance uPVC fascias and soffits — factory finished, never needs painting.

Gutter Clearing

Moss from north-facing slopes, leaf debris and silt all clog uPVC gutters. We clear, flush and check fall angles — a fast job that prevents damp problems below.

Downpipe Repair

Cracked or leaking downpipes, blocked offsets and failed joints at the shoe — repaired or replaced as needed. We also check the connection to the underground drainage run.

Seamless Aluminium Guttering

For Hall Green's larger detached properties, seamless aluminium guttering is a premium option — extruded on-site to the exact run length, no joints, no leaks.

Roof Edge Detailing

Correct drip edge and lead flashing at the eaves keeps water off the fascia and into the gutter. We check and correct the roof edge detail as part of any full gutter replacement.

Guttering Across B28

Hall Green's 1930s properties typically had their original cast iron guttering replaced with uPVC in the 1980s and 90s — and that uPVC is now 30–40 years old and starting to fail. Joints brittle and cracking, brackets pulling from ageing fascias, and sections sagging under the weight of accumulated debris.

Full uPVC gutter replacement is usually a half-day job on a typical Hall Green semi. We replace gutters, fascia boards and soffits as a package where needed — a clean result that protects the roofline for another 20+ years.

Signs your guttering needs attention

  • Damp on the wall below the gutterline
  • Gutters overflowing in rain
  • Brittle or cracked uPVC sections
  • Sagging gutter runs
  • Rotten or soft fascia board
  • Gutters pulling away from the wall
