Guttering in Druids Heath

Druids Heath's estate properties were fitted with uPVC guttering in the 1980s–90s as part of improvements to the original 1960s–70s stock. That uPVC is now 30–40 years old and starting to fail — brittle joints, brackets pulling from ageing fascia boards, and sections sagging and overflowing. Full gutter replacement is usually a half-day job on a typical estate semi, and it makes a significant difference to keeping the roofline watertight.

Signs your guttering needs attention

  • Overflowing in heavy rain
  • Cracked or split uPVC sections
  • Sagging gutter runs
  • Damp patches below the gutterline
  • Rotten or soft fascia boards
  • Gutters pulling away from the wall
