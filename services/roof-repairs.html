Don't Ignore These

Signs You Need a Roof Repair

Caught early, most repairs are quick and affordable. Left too long, small problems become expensive ones.

💧

Water stains on ceilings

Brown or damp patches on ceilings are almost always a sign of a roof leak above.

🪨

Missing or broken tiles

A single displaced tile exposes the felt beneath and lets water in within weeks.

🌿

Moss & algae growth

Heavy moss holds moisture against tiles and breaks down the surface over time.

🔩

Cracked or loose flashings

Flashings around chimneys, skylights and valleys are a common entry point for leaks.

🧱

Crumbling mortar on ridge

Ridge tiles bedded in old mortar loosen over time — they can fall off in high winds.

🌫️

Damp or musty loft

Persistent damp smell in your loft often means water is getting in at a small point above.

What We Repair

Types of Roof Repair We Cover

Broken or Missing Tiles & Slates

We source matching tiles or slates and replace them correctly — re-nailing, re-pointing and checking the surrounding area for any further movement. We don't just slot a tile in and leave; we inspect what's around it too.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Even one missing tile exposes the felt beneath to the elements. Felt degrades quickly once wet and UV-exposed. Within weeks water begins soaking into the roof timbers, saturating insulation and causing rot in joists and rafters. You'll see damp stains on ceilings — by which point the damage is already done and the repair bill is significantly higher.

From £150

Ridge & Hip Tile Re-bedding

Ridge and hip tiles are bedded in mortar along the very top of your roof. Over time that mortar cracks and crumbles, leaving tiles loose. We re-bed them in fresh mortar and re-point the entire run to create a proper weatherproof seal.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Loose ridge tiles are a serious safety hazard — in high winds they can become dislodged and fall. A ridge tile falling from height can injure people below, damage cars, or break through a conservatory roof. Beyond the safety risk, the exposed gap lets water pour directly into the roof void, quickly rotting the timbers beneath. This is one of the most common causes of major roof damage in Birmingham.

From £250

Flashing Repairs & Replacement

Flashings are the lead or metal seals at every junction where your roof meets a wall, chimney, skylight or valley. We repair or replace lead step flashings, apron flashings and valley linings — getting a watertight seal that lasts.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Failed flashing is one of the most common hidden causes of damp in Birmingham homes. Water tracks silently behind the flashing and into the wall cavity or straight onto a ceiling — often showing up as damp patches on interior walls rather than an obvious drip. By the time you notice it, the damage to plasterwork, insulation and timber framing can be extensive and expensive to put right.

From £200

Chimney Stack Repairs

Chimneys take the worst of the weather all year round. We repair cracked pointing, replace deteriorated flaunching (the cement crown), re-lead the flashings and cap unused flues — covering every part of the stack properly.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Open mortar joints absorb water which then freezes and expands in winter, rapidly accelerating cracking. Left long enough, this leads to loose bricks, a structurally unstable stack and ultimately the risk of collapse. A fallen chimney stack causes catastrophic damage to the roof below and is extremely dangerous. Rebuilding a collapsed chimney costs several times more than maintaining it.

From £300

Leak Tracing & Repair

The damp patch on your ceiling is rarely directly below the entry point — water travels. We carry out a proper inspection to find where it's actually getting in, then fix the cause. No guesswork, no temporary patches that fail three months later.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

An untraced leak continues silently, often spreading to affect a wider area of insulation, roof timbers and ceilings. Many homeowners get repeat patch fixes that never address the real problem, spending more in total than a single proper repair would have cost. In severe cases, prolonged water ingress causes mould growth throughout the loft space and can lead to ceiling collapse.

From £150

Moss Removal & Treatment

We remove moss safely using soft brushing (not high-pressure washing, which damages tiles) then apply a professional biocide treatment that kills the root systems and prevents regrowth for several years.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Moss acts like a sponge — it holds moisture against the tile surface continuously, breaking down the surface over time. Moss roots also work between tiles, gradually lifting them and opening gaps. As it dies and washes off, it blocks gutters and downpipes, causing water to overflow against the fascia and wall. This leads to damp walls, rotting fascia boards and can void manufacturer warranties on concrete or clay tiles.

From £200

Valley Repairs

Roof valleys are the channels where two roof slopes meet — they carry the highest volume of water on the entire roof. We repair or replace lead-lined and mortar-bedded valleys to make them fully watertight again.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

A failing valley funnels large amounts of water directly into the roof structure every time it rains. Because the volume is high, damage accumulates fast — saturated insulation, rotting timbers and ceiling damage can occur across a wide area of the house. Valley problems are often hidden for months before showing internally, by which point extensive hidden rot may already be present.

From £350

Partial Roof Re-Covering

When a significant section of your roof has deteriorated beyond spot repairs — failed felt, rotten battens or widespread tile damage — we strip the area back completely and re-cover it properly with new membrane, battens and tiles.

⚠️ If left unrepaired

Deteriorated felt and rotten battens allow moisture ingress even through tiles that appear intact on the surface. Left too long, the rot spreads to adjacent sections and into the structural rafters below. What could have been a contained partial repair becomes a full roof replacement — at two to three times the cost. Acting early keeps the work manageable and the bill significantly lower.

From £800
