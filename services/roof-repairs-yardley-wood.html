Roof Repair Services in Yardley Wood B14

Yardley Wood's 1930s semis share the same roofing characteristics as much of south Birmingham — hipped roofs, concrete interlocking tiles (often replacements from the 1960s–70s), and ridge tiles bedded in sand/cement that has carbonated over the decades. Hip tile re-bedding and ridge tile re-pointing are the two most common repairs we carry out in B14, and Yardley Wood sees its fair share of both.

Cracked Tile Replacement

Concrete tiles on 1930s properties crack and delaminate with age. We replace damaged tiles on a like-for-like basis, sourcing matching profiles where available.

Hipped Roof Re-bedding

Hip junctions on Yardley Wood's 1930s semis require mortar re-bedding as the original compound carbonates. We use modern flexible mortar that lasts significantly longer.

Ridge Re-bedding

Failed mortar on ridge tiles is the most common repair we carry out in Yardley Wood. Re-bedded and re-pointed properly — not just surface-patched with filler.

Lead Flashing Repairs

Step and apron flashings on chimney stacks and flat roof abutments repaired or replaced in code lead. We seal flashing correctly, not with mastic or sealant.

Chimney Repairs

Repointing, flaunching repair and flashing replacement on Yardley Wood's original chimney stacks. A failing chimney is a common source of water ingress on 1930s properties.

Leak Diagnosis

Not sure where the water is getting in? We trace and diagnose roof leaks accurately — a vital first step before any repair work on older properties with complex roof structures.
